## What is Kisspeptin-10?

**Kisspeptin-10** (Metastin 45-54) is the minimal bioactive C-terminal decapeptide fragment of the KISS1 gene product. It is the most potent endogenous ligand for the KISS1R receptor (formerly GPR54) and serves as a critical upstream regulator of gonadotropin-releasing hormone (GnRH) neuron activity, making it indispensable for studying reproductive neuroendocrinology and puberty mechanisms. For published research, see [PubMed studies on Kisspeptin-10](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/?term=kisspeptin-10){:target="_blank" rel="noopener"}.

The discovery of kisspeptin signalling revolutionised reproductive biology, establishing it as the essential gatekeeper for GnRH secretion and puberty initiation in mammals.
