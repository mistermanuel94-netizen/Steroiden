## What is Dihexa?

**Dihexa** (N-hexanoic-Tyr-Ile-(6)-aminohexanoic amide, also known as PNB-0408) is a small, orally active hexapeptide analogue developed by researchers Joseph Harding and John Wright at Washington State University. Dihexa acts as an allosteric modulator of hepatocyte growth factor (HGF), dramatically potentiating its activation of the c-Met receptor. For published research, see [PubMed studies on Dihexa](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/?term=dihexa+HGF+cognitive){:target="_blank" rel="noopener"}.

Dihexa is one of the most potent synaptogenic compounds described in the literature, estimated to be approximately 10⁷ times more potent than brain-derived neurotrophic factor (BDNF) in promoting new synapse formation.
