## What is Thymalin?

**Thymalin** is a bioregulator peptide complex originally isolated from bovine thymus glands, developed by Professor Vladimir Khavinson at the Saint Petersburg Institute of Bioregulation and Gerontology. It is the immune system-targeted member of the Khavinson bioregulator peptide family, studied for its ability to modulate T-lymphocyte differentiation, restore thymic function, and counteract age-related immunosenescence. For published research, see [PubMed studies on Thymalin](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/?term=thymalin+thymic+peptide+immune){:target="_blank" rel="noopener"}.

Thymalin has a long research history spanning several decades, with studies investigating its role in T-cell homeostasis and thymic involution reversal in ageing models.
