## What is Pancragen?

**Pancragen** is a synthetic bioregulatory tetrapeptide with the sequence Lys-Glu-Asp-Trp (KEDW), developed as part of Professor Vladimir Khavinson's bioregulatory peptide programme at the St. Petersburg Institute of Bioregulation and Gerontology. Pancragen specifically targets pancreatic tissue, modulating gene expression in islet beta-cells to promote insulin synthesis, glucose homeostasis, and protection against metabolic and inflammatory stress. For published research, see [PubMed studies on Pancragen](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/?term=pancragen+bioregulator+peptide+pancreatic){:target="_blank" rel="noopener"}.

Pancragen is an important research tool for studying pancreatic bioregulation, beta-cell function, metabolic disease models, and the effects of short peptides on endocrine biology.
