## What is GDF-8 Propeptide?

**GDF-8 Propeptide** (Myostatin Propeptide) is the naturally occurring inhibitory prodomain of myostatin (Growth Differentiation Factor-8), a TGF-β superfamily member that serves as the primary negative regulator of skeletal muscle mass. The propeptide maintains myostatin in a latent, inactive state through non-covalent complex formation, representing the body's endogenous mechanism for controlling myostatin activity. For published research, see [PubMed studies on GDF-8 Propeptide](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/?term=myostatin+propeptide+GDF-8){:target="_blank" rel="noopener"}.

Understanding the propeptide-myostatin latent complex is central to myostatin biology and has informed the development of therapeutic strategies for muscle wasting conditions.
