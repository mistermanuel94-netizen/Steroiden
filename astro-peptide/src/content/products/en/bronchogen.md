## What is Bronchogen?

**Bronchogen** is a synthetic bioregulatory tetrapeptide with the sequence Ala-Glu-Asp-Leu (AEDL), developed as part of Professor Vladimir Khavinson's bioregulatory peptide programme at the St. Petersburg Institute of Bioregulation and Gerontology. Bronchogen specifically targets bronchial tissue, modulating gene expression in airway epithelial cells to promote respiratory function, mucociliary clearance, and protection from inflammatory damage. For published research, see [PubMed studies on Bronchogen](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/?term=bronchogen+bioregulator+peptide+respiratory){:target="_blank" rel="noopener"}.

Bronchogen is an important research tool for studying bronchial bioregulation, chronic respiratory conditions, and the effects of short peptides on airway biology.
