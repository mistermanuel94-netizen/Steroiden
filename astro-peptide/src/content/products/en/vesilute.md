## What is Vesilute?

**Vesilute** is a synthetic bioregulatory tetrapeptide developed as part of Professor Vladimir Khavinson's bioregulatory peptide programme at the St. Petersburg Institute of Bioregulation and Gerontology. Vesilute is a Lys-Glu-Asp-Trp (KEDW) variant that specifically targets bladder and urinary tract tissue, modulating gene expression in urothelial cells and detrusor smooth muscle to promote bladder function, urothelial barrier integrity, and protection against inflammatory and age-related urinary changes. For published research, see [PubMed studies on Vesilute](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/?term=vesilute+bioregulator+peptide+bladder){:target="_blank" rel="noopener"}.

Vesilute is an important research tool for studying bladder bioregulation, urinary tract ageing, overactive bladder mechanisms, and the effects of short peptides on urological biology.
