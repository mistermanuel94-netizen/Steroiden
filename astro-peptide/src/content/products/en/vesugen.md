## What is Vesugen?

**Vesugen** is a synthetic bioregulatory tripeptide with the sequence Lys-Glu-Asp (KED), developed as part of Professor Vladimir Khavinson's bioregulatory peptide programme at the St. Petersburg Institute of Bioregulation and Gerontology. Vesugen specifically targets vascular tissue, modulating gene expression in endothelial cells and vascular smooth muscle to promote endothelial function, vascular wall integrity, and protection against atherosclerotic and age-related cardiovascular changes. For published research, see [PubMed studies on Vesugen](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/?term=vesugen+bioregulator+peptide+vascular){:target="_blank" rel="noopener"}.

Vesugen is an important research tool for studying vascular bioregulation, endothelial dysfunction, cardiovascular ageing, and the effects of short peptides on vascular biology.
