## What is Ovagen?

**Ovagen** is a synthetic bioregulatory tripeptide with the sequence Lys-Glu-Asp (KED), developed as part of Professor Vladimir Khavinson's bioregulatory peptide programme at the St. Petersburg Institute of Bioregulation and Gerontology. Ovagen specifically targets hepatic tissue, modulating gene expression in hepatocytes to promote liver function, metabolic homeostasis, and tissue repair. For published research, see [PubMed studies on Ovagen](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/?term=ovagen+bioregulator+peptide+liver){:target="_blank" rel="noopener"}.

Ovagen is a key research tool for studying hepatic bioregulation, liver ageing, and the effects of short peptides on hepatocyte function and liver metabolism.
