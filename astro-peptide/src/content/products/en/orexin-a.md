## What is Orexin-A?

**Orexin-A** (Hypocretin-1) is a 33-amino acid excitatory neuropeptide produced by a small population of neurons in the lateral hypothalamus. It contains two intramolecular disulfide bonds (Cys⁶-Cys¹² and Cys⁷-Cys¹⁴) and an N-terminal pyroglutamyl residue, making it more structurally complex and metabolically stable than Orexin-B. For published research, see [PubMed studies on Orexin-A](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/?term=orexin-A+hypocretin-1){:target="_blank" rel="noopener"}.

The orexin system is a master regulator of wakefulness, and loss of orexin-producing neurons is the primary cause of type 1 narcolepsy. Orexin-A also plays critical roles in feeding behaviour, energy homeostasis, and reward processing.
