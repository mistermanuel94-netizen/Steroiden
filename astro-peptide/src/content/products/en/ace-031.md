## What is ACE-031?

**ACE-031** (ACVR2B-Fc) is a soluble activin receptor type IIB-Fc fusion protein that functions as a ligand trap for myostatin (GDF-8), activin A, GDF-11, and other TGF-β superfamily members. By sequestering these negative regulators of muscle mass, ACE-031 provides a powerful research tool for studying muscle growth signalling, neuromuscular biology, and metabolic regulation. For published research, see [PubMed studies on ACE-031](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/?term=ACE-031+ACVR2B){:target="_blank" rel="noopener"}.

ACE-031 was originally developed as a therapeutic candidate for Duchenne muscular dystrophy and has since become an important reagent in muscle biology and TGF-β pathway research.
