## What is Vilon?

**Vilon** is a synthetic bioregulatory dipeptide with the sequence Lys-Glu (KE), developed as part of Professor Vladimir Khavinson's bioregulatory peptide programme at the St. Petersburg Institute of Bioregulation and Gerontology. Vilon is the shortest known bioactive peptide in the Khavinson series, consisting of only two amino acids, yet it demonstrates significant immunomodulatory activity. It specifically targets thymic tissue, modulating gene expression in thymocytes and thymic epithelial cells to promote T-cell maturation, immune reconstitution, and reversal of age-related thymic involution. For published research, see [PubMed studies on Vilon](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/?term=vilon+dipeptide+bioregulator+immune+thymic){:target="_blank" rel="noopener"}.

Vilon is an important research tool for studying thymic bioregulation, immune senescence, minimal-length bioactive peptide mechanisms, and the effects of ultra-short peptides on immunological biology.
