## What is Matrixyl?

**Matrixyl** (Palmitoyl Pentapeptide-4, Pal-KTTKS) is a lipopeptide consisting of the pentapeptide sequence Lys-Thr-Thr-Lys-Ser conjugated to palmitic acid. The KTTKS sequence is derived from the pro-collagen I C-terminal propeptide and acts as a matrikine – a matrix-derived signalling molecule that stimulates extracellular matrix protein synthesis in dermal fibroblasts. For published research, see [PubMed studies on Matrixyl](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/?term=palmitoyl+pentapeptide+KTTKS+collagen){:target="_blank" rel="noopener"}.

Matrixyl is one of the most clinically validated cosmeceutical peptides, with published studies demonstrating significant collagen stimulation and wrinkle reduction in both in vitro and clinical settings.
