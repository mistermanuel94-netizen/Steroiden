## What is MOTS-c?

**MOTS-c** (Mitochondrial ORF of the 12S rRNA Type-c) is a mitochondrial-derived peptide (MDP) encoded within the mitochondrial genome's 12S rRNA gene. Discovered in 2015, it represents a revolutionary link between mitochondrial and nuclear communication, functioning as a retrograde signal that regulates metabolic homeostasis. For published research, see [PubMed studies on MOTS-c](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/?term=MOTS-c){:target="_blank" rel="noopener"}.

MOTS-c has been called an "exercise mimetic" for its ability to activate AMPK and promote metabolic benefits similar to physical exercise, making it one of the most exciting peptides in ageing and metabolic research.
