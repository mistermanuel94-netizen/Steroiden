## What is MGF?

**MGF** (Mechano Growth Factor) is a splice variant of insulin-like growth factor 1 (IGF-1Ec in humans, IGF-1Eb in rodents) produced in response to mechanical loading and tissue damage. MGF features a unique C-terminal E-domain peptide (24 amino acids) that distinguishes it from mature IGF-1 and is responsible for its distinct biological activities, particularly satellite cell activation and early-phase repair signalling. For published research, see [PubMed studies on MGF](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/?term=mechano+growth+factor+MGF){:target="_blank" rel="noopener"}.

MGF is a key research tool for studying the mechanotransduction pathways linking mechanical stress to tissue repair and regeneration.
