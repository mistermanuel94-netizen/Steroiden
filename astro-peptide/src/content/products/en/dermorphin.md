## What is Dermorphin?

**Dermorphin** (Tyr-D-Ala-Phe-Gly-Tyr-Pro-Ser-NH₂) is a naturally occurring heptapeptide originally isolated from the skin secretions of South American tree frogs (*Phyllomedusa sauvagei*). It is one of the most potent and selective μ-opioid receptor (MOR) agonists known, exhibiting approximately 40-fold greater affinity than morphine. For published research, see [PubMed studies on Dermorphin](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/?term=dermorphin+opioid+receptor){:target="_blank" rel="noopener"}.

Notably, Dermorphin contains a D-alanine residue at position 2—an extremely rare occurrence in naturally derived peptides—which confers exceptional resistance to enzymatic degradation and is critical for its potent receptor binding.
