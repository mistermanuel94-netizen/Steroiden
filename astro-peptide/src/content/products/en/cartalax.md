## What is Cartalax?

**Cartalax** is a synthetic bioregulatory tripeptide with the sequence Ala-Glu-Asp (AED), developed as part of Professor Vladimir Khavinson's bioregulatory peptide programme at the St. Petersburg Institute of Bioregulation and Gerontology. Cartalax specifically targets cartilage and bone tissue, modulating gene expression in chondrocytes and osteoblasts to promote extracellular matrix synthesis, chondroprotection, and skeletal tissue maintenance. Notably, Cartalax has also demonstrated anti-ageing properties through telomere length preservation and epigenetic regulation. For published research, see [PubMed studies on Cartalax](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/?term=cartalax+bioregulator+peptide+cartilage){:target="_blank" rel="noopener"}.

Cartalax is an important research tool for studying cartilage bioregulation, musculoskeletal ageing, anti-ageing epigenetics, and the effects of short peptides on connective tissue biology.
