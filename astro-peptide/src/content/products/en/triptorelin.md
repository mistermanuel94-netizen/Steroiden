## What is Triptorelin?

**Triptorelin** is a synthetic decapeptide analogue of gonadotropin-releasing hormone (GnRH) with a D-Trp substitution at position 6, conferring enhanced potency and metabolic stability compared to endogenous GnRH. It is widely employed as a research tool for investigating hypothalamic-pituitary-gonadal (HPG) axis regulation, GnRH receptor pharmacology, and reproductive endocrinology. For published research, see [PubMed studies on Triptorelin](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/?term=triptorelin){:target="_blank" rel="noopener"}.

Triptorelin acts by initially stimulating and subsequently desensitising pituitary GnRH receptors, producing a biphasic "flare-then-block" response that has made it invaluable for studying receptor downregulation mechanisms.
