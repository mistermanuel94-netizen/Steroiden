## What is Orexin-B?

**Orexin-B** (Hypocretin-2) is a 28-amino acid linear neuropeptide produced by lateral hypothalamic neurons alongside Orexin-A. Both peptides are derived from a common precursor, prepro-orexin, but Orexin-B lacks the disulfide bonds and pyroglutamyl cap of Orexin-A, giving it a distinct receptor selectivity profile. For published research, see [PubMed studies on Orexin-B](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/?term=orexin-B+hypocretin-2){:target="_blank" rel="noopener"}.

Orexin-B shows approximately 10-fold preferential binding to the OX2 receptor, which is strongly expressed in wake-promoting centres such as the tuberomammillary nucleus. This receptor selectivity makes Orexin-B a valuable probe for dissecting the contributions of OX2R versus OX1R to wakefulness and other orexin-regulated functions.
