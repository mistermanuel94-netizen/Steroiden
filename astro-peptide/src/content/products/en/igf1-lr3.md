## What is IGF-1 LR3?

**IGF-1 LR3** (Long Arginine 3-IGF-1) is a modified analogue of insulin-like growth factor 1, engineered with a 13-amino acid N-terminal extension peptide and an arginine substitution at position 3. These modifications dramatically reduce binding to IGF binding proteins (IGFBPs), resulting in a significantly longer half-life and greater bioavailability compared to native IGF-1. For published research, see [PubMed studies on IGF-1 LR3](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/?term=IGF-1+LR3){:target="_blank" rel="noopener"}.

As a potent growth factor, IGF-1 LR3 is widely used in cell biology and tissue engineering research for its ability to promote cell proliferation and survival.
