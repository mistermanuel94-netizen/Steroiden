## What is Adamax?

**Adamax** (N-Acetyl Semax Amidate) is an enhanced synthetic analogue of Semax, itself derived from the ACTH(4-10) fragment. Adamax features two key structural modifications—N-terminal acetylation and C-terminal amidation—that significantly improve metabolic stability and blood-brain barrier penetrance compared to unmodified Semax. For published research, see [PubMed studies on Semax analogues](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/?term=semax+N-acetyl+amidate){:target="_blank" rel="noopener"}.

These modifications make Adamax a potent research tool for studying neurotrophic factor modulation, cognitive enhancement pathways, and neuroprotective mechanisms in preclinical models.
