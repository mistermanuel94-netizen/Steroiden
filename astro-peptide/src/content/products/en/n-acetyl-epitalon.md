## What is N-Acetyl Epitalon?

**N-Acetyl Epitalon** (N-Acetyl Epithalon Amidate, Ac-AEDG-NH₂) is a chemically enhanced variant of the telomerase-activating tetrapeptide Epitalon. The N-terminal acetylation and C-terminal amidation modifications confer improved metabolic stability, enhanced cellular permeability, and extended biological half-life compared to the unmodified AEDG peptide. For published research, see [PubMed studies on Epitalon](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/?term=epitalon+epithalon+telomerase){:target="_blank" rel="noopener"}.

Originally derived from research on epithalamin, a pineal gland extract studied extensively by Professor Vladimir Khavinson, N-Acetyl Epitalon represents a next-generation tool for telomere biology and cellular ageing research.
