## What is FTTP/Adipotide?

**FTTP/Adipotide** is a chimeric proapoptotic peptide designed to selectively target the vasculature of white adipose tissue. It consists of two functional domains: a targeting motif (CKGGRAKDC) that binds prohibitin on adipose tissue blood vessel endothelium, and a proapoptotic sequence D(KLAKLAK)₂ that disrupts mitochondrial membranes upon cellular internalisation. For published research, see [PubMed studies on Adipotide](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/?term=adipotide+prohibitin+peptide){:target="_blank" rel="noopener"}.

Originally characterised in primate models of obesity, FTTP/Adipotide represents a novel approach to studying targeted vascular disruption of adipose tissue and has become a valuable tool in obesity and metabolic research.
