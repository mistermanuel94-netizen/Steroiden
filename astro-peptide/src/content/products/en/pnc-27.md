## What is PNC-27?

**PNC-27** is a synthetic anti-cancer research peptide containing a p53 C-terminal–derived binding domain linked to a cell-penetrating leader sequence. PNC-27 is designed to target and disrupt the interaction between HDM-2 (human double minute 2) and p53 at the surface of transformed cells. Unlike conventional cytotoxic agents, PNC-27 exhibits selective membranolysis toward cancer cells that overexpress HDM-2 on their surface, while leaving non-cancerous cells unharmed. For published research, see [PubMed studies on PNC-27](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/?term=PNC-27+anti-cancer+peptide){:target="_blank" rel="noopener"}.

PNC-27's unique mechanism of action—targeting surface-bound HDM-2 rather than intracellular pathways—makes it an important tool for oncology research.
