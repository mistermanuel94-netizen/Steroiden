## What is Oxytocin?

**Oxytocin** is a cyclic nonapeptide hormone and neurotransmitter produced in the hypothalamus and released by the posterior pituitary. It is one of the most extensively studied neuropeptides, with roles spanning reproduction, social behaviour, emotional regulation, and neuroendocrine function. For published research, see [PubMed studies on Oxytocin](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/?term=oxytocin+peptide){:target="_blank" rel="noopener"}.

Its dual function as both a peripheral hormone and central neurotransmitter makes Oxytocin uniquely valuable for research across endocrinology, neuroscience, and behavioural science.
