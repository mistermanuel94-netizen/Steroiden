## What is IGF-1 DES?

**IGF-1 DES** (Des(1-3) IGF-1) is a truncated analogue of insulin-like growth factor 1 lacking the first three N-terminal amino acids (Gly-Pro-Glu). This structural modification significantly reduces binding affinity to IGF binding proteins (IGFBPs), resulting in dramatically enhanced bioavailability and approximately 10-fold greater potency in cell proliferation assays compared to native IGF-1. For published research, see [PubMed studies on IGF-1 DES](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/?term=Des(1-3)+IGF-1){:target="_blank" rel="noopener"}.

IGF-1 DES is a critical research tool for studying IGF-1 receptor (IGF-1R) signalling independent of IGFBP regulation, enabling researchers to isolate direct receptor-mediated effects.
