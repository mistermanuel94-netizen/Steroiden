## What is Pinealon?

**Pinealon** (EDR, Glu-Asp-Arg) is a synthetic bioregulator tripeptide developed from pineal gland research by Professor Vladimir Khavinson and colleagues at the Saint Petersburg Institute of Bioregulation and Gerontology. It belongs to the class of short regulatory peptides hypothesised to modulate gene expression in CNS tissue, with reported neuroprotective and antioxidant properties. For published research, see [PubMed studies on Pinealon](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/?term=pinealon+EDR+peptide+neuroprotective){:target="_blank" rel="noopener"}.

Pinealon is the brain-targeted member of the Khavinson bioregulator peptide family and is studied for its potential to protect neurons from oxidative damage and modulate gene expression in neural tissue.
