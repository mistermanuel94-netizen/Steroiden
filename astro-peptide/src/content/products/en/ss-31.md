## What is SS-31?

**SS-31** (Elamipretide, also known as Bendavia or MTP-131) is a cell-permeable, mitochondria-targeted tetrapeptide (D-Arg-dimethylTyr-Lys-Phe-NH₂) developed by Hazel Szeto and Peter Bhatt Schiller. SS-31 selectively concentrates at the inner mitochondrial membrane through its affinity for cardiolipin, a phospholipid essential for electron transport chain function. For published research, see [PubMed studies on SS-31](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/?term=SS-31+elamipretide){:target="_blank" rel="noopener"}.

SS-31 is one of the most extensively studied mitochondria-targeted peptides, with applications in cardioprotection, neurodegeneration, and age-related mitochondrial dysfunction research.
