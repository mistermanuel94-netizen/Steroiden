## What is Cardiogen?

**Cardiogen** is a synthetic bioregulatory tetrapeptide with the sequence Ala-Glu-Asp-Arg (AEDR), developed as part of Professor Vladimir Khavinson's bioregulatory peptide programme at the St. Petersburg Institute of Bioregulation and Gerontology. Cardiogen specifically targets cardiac tissue, modulating gene expression in cardiomyocytes to promote myocardial protection and repair. For published research, see [PubMed studies on Cardiogen](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/?term=cardiogen+bioregulator+peptide){:target="_blank" rel="noopener"}.

Cardiogen represents an important research tool for studying cardiac bioregulation, ischaemic heart disease mechanisms, and cardiomyocyte biology.
