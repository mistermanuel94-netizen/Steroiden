## What is PTD-DBM?

**PTD-DBM** (Protein Transduction Domain-Dishevelled Binding Motif) is a cell-penetrating peptide designed to activate the Wnt/β-catenin signalling pathway by disrupting the inhibitory interaction between CXXC5 and Dishevelled (Dvl). This mechanism releases the endogenous brake on Wnt signalling, promoting downstream transcription of genes involved in hair follicle neogenesis and tissue regeneration. For published research, see [PubMed studies on PTD-DBM](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/?term=PTD-DBM+Wnt+hair){:target="_blank" rel="noopener"}.

Originally developed as a research tool for studying Wnt pathway biology, PTD-DBM has attracted significant attention for its potential applications in hair follicle regeneration and dermal papilla cell biology.
