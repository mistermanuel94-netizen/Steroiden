## What is PE-22-28?

**PE-22-28** is a synthetic heptapeptide derived from the phosphodiesterase 4 (PDE4)-binding domain of RACK1 (Receptor for Activated C Kinase 1). By disrupting the RACK1-PDE4 protein-protein interaction, PE-22-28 inhibits PDE4-mediated cAMP degradation, leading to elevated intracellular cAMP and enhanced PKA/CREB signalling—a molecular cascade fundamental to memory formation and synaptic plasticity. For published research, see [PubMed studies on PE-22-28](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/?term=PE-22-28+PDE4+RACK1+cognitive){:target="_blank" rel="noopener"}.

PE-22-28 represents a novel peptide-based approach to PDE4 inhibition that may avoid the emetic side effects associated with small-molecule PDE4 inhibitors like rolipram.
